About Gentrification in the Bay

What is gentrification?

Gentrification is a process of renovation of deteriorated urban neighborhoods by means of the influx of more affluent residents.

Rising house prices

Increasing housing prices force communities apart and individuals from their homes.

Rapid movement of capital

All progress comes at a cost. As incomes rise and education improves for some, others lose access to opportunities and affordable housing.


Tech boom

Tech companies in San Francisco (SF) have the responsibility to all people in San Francisco, not just their employees.

Gentrification is a regional issue

Cities such as Menlo Park, Palo Alto, and Mountain View all have the power to exacerbate and mitigate the effects of gentrification in San Francisco.

Disproportionate Impact

Gentrification disproportionately impacts minorities and historically marginalized groups.

What you can do

Vote

Reform Proposition 13: A proposed state constitutional amendment would split Proposition 13, which would keep existing taxes in place on residential buildings, but allow increasing taxes on other types of properties. Higher taxes on commercial properties would generate billions in funds for to be reinvested in the community.

Repeal Costa-Hawkins Law on the November ballot: The law currently prohibits California cities from applying rent control to buildings constructed after 1978. Repealing this law would allow cities to provide more affordable housing for tenants and decrease displacement.

Fight Ellis Act Eviction: Under this new law, landlords can evict their tenants under little regulation.

Get Involved

Encourage your local government and your employer to build more affordable housing. Advocate for zoning laws that protect culturally significant areas, support legislation that improves public transport, and be cognizant of the was your lifestyle has an impact on the communities around you.

Spread Awareness

Talk to your friends about the importance of this issue. Push back against your employers to protect communities in which your company inhabits.
